May 2025 Recap

Testing efforts on the DarkAero 1 continued in May, with a focus on characterizing bonded structures and validating the wing and fuselage structural behavior under load. New technicians were added to the DarkAero team, more customer projects were shipped, and another session of the DarkAero Aerospace Composites Course was held.

DarkAero 1 Test Campaign
Work continued on updates to the DarkAero 1 wing that started in March. Recent data collected from full-scale testing is being used to optimize the wing center section modifications that are underway. These include a fuel line pass through, additional structure to stiffen the flap system controls, and new honeycomb sandwich panel construction in the shear webs that support the wing skins. In parallel, coupon-level tests are being conducted to explore candidate assembly architectures that will be incorporated into the wing updates. These efforts are helping to prove out the flight-ready configuration while enhancing the structural margin of the wing under a wide range of loading conditions.

Given the highly optimized design of the DarkAero 1, these tests are critical to meet the structural requirements and efficiency targets of the aircraft. The results obtained during this test phase are contributing directly to design decisions that balance weight, strength, and manufacturability.

Increased Test Capability
To accelerate iteration and gain more detailed insight into materials and structures, the team began leveraging a new test machine that was brought in-house in May. Previous testing had been accomplished using test equipment at external labs, but having this machine in-house now allows for faster iteration and more flexibility with coupon testing. It also enables more thorough evaluation of new adhesives, resins, materials, and bonding procedures. Investing in this internal capability reflects the broader strategy of detailed and thorough engineering and testing of DarkAero hardware. This internal capability also has allowed further refinement and optimization of customer hardware.

DarkAero Courses
Session 25 of the DarkAero Aerospace Composite Course was held in May. Students came with challenging composite projects from commercial EVTOLs to seaplanes. Students participated in a variety of hands-on demos mixed with whiteboard discussions to ultimately help them accelerate progress on their projects. This session featured a new demo of a 3-point bend test that allowed students to see quantified measurements of the mechanical properties of composite material test samples they produced in class.
