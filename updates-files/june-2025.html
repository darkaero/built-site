June 2025 Recap

Progress continued on the DarkAero 1 prototype in June, with efforts focused on building molds and structures that will be incorporated into the center section of the wing. Additional manufacturing equipment was acquired and more customer projects were delivered.
DarkAero Manufacturing Engineer, Gage Kuszmaul, inspecting the pi joint mold assembly for the DarkAero 1 prototype wing. 

Airframe Testing
Work continued on implementing several structural modifications in the center section of the wing. While the wing was previously proof load tested under both positive and negative G load conditions, proof load testing of the flap and aileron systems uncovered portions of the mounting structure that required additional stiffness to minimize control system deflection. Since the flap actuation mechanism is mounted to the shear webs in the center section of the wing, these sandwich panel structures required modification. A number of other proposed improvements were developed in the process of building and testing the wing, so these were batched in with the center section modification work as well. 
CAD render of the completed pi joint mold assembly. 

One of the more substantial modifications involves the addition of pi joints between the center shear webs and the wing skins. Pi joints, so named because their cross section resembles the Greek letter Ï€, are a type of bonded joint that provides high strength in out-of-plane loading. This is particularly beneficial in the wing center section as a potential failure mode of the wing structure stems from buckling of the wing skins leading to out-of-plane loading of the shear web joints. The addition of pi joints increases the structural margin between the wing limit load and the buckling load of the wing skins. Pi joints are also recognized in aerospace as less sensitive to manufacturing variability and are more durable under load compared to legacy joint configurations. 
Section view of a pi joint sample that was tested.

Coupon testing was conducted to quantify the strength and characterize the failure modes of pi joints created with the specific materials, dimensions, and manufacturing processes that are planned for the wing modification. The testing also quantified the bond strength of the pi joint flanges using the planned adhesive and bonding procedures. In parallel, testing was conducted to validate the bonding procedure itself prior to installing the pi joint assembly into the lower wing skin. Bonding parameters that were under evaluation included the amount of adhesive, adhesive application pattern, bond-line thickness, and fixturing methods. After the testing was complete, efforts shifted to manufacturing the molds for the precured pi joints. 
Timelapse of mold risers being created for the pi joint mold assembly. 

Three multisection molds were built to create the pi joint structures, with the base of the molds CNC machined from a high-temperature-capable tooling board. The portion of the mold used to form the vertical flanges of the pi joints was additively manufactured. These molds will be used to create prepreg pi joint assemblies which will be post cured at elevated temperature under vacuum. After cure they will be trimmed to final dimensions and bonded into the wing.
Precision ground surface of the new steel flat plate table.

Manufacturing Tools
To support the production of large composite panels for DarkAero 1 kits, a custom-built, precision-ground steel flat plate table was added to the tools in the shop. Beyond panel production, this table will provide a flat reference surface for assembling and bonding large-scale components with the tolerances necessary for high-performance flight hardware.
