August 2021 Recap

Progress on the DarkAero 1 prototype continued in August with the team focused on manufacturing more main landing gear components as well as the integration of the electrical system hardware into the airframe.
Overview of all main landing gear components completed to date.

Main Gear
The main landing gear represents one of the last outstanding systems to complete in the aircraft, so naturally we feel eager to bring this portion of the design to completion. Efforts on the main gear continued in August as we CNC machined many of the parts that make up the load bearing structures in this assembly. Both of the drag link subassemblies to support the left and right gear struts were completed. These parts are identical from left to right, which simplifies the manufacturing, both in a prototype and production environment. We also milled the strut brackets that tie the drag links to the carbon fiber gear struts. The carbon fiber parts that form the trunnion at the top of the main gear strut were machined as well. We are in the process of milling the strut bottoms, which join the trailing links to the struts and establish the camber and toe-in angle of the wheels. The main gear assembly will be joined to the fuselage through a molded carbon fiber structure that will distribute the landing loads into the rest of the airframe. We are finalizing the mold design for this structure and are looking forward to building it in September. 
View from below of the mounted cockpit wiring.

Electrical System
Integration of the electrical system into the airframe continued this past month. An exciting milestone was reached when we fully connected the instrument panel to the engine through the central tunnel avionics box. This allowed us to communicate with the engine and perform several basic system function checks including toggling the fuel pump relays, changing the propeller pitch from inside the cockpit, and reading sensor data from the CHT and EGT sensors. Installation of the wiring harness from the firewall through the cabin and back to the aft electronics module was another project we worked on. This involved installing Click Bond studs and loop clamps to constrain the wire harness. The path followed by the wiring harness was strategically planned out to give adequate separation around obstructions such as fuel lines and control system hardware. We also installed a few more sensors to further instrument the engine for flight testing.
Recently installed right wing tip cap along with the pitot tube mount.

Wing Tips
Additional work on the electrical system installation took place in the wing tips of the aircraft. The left tip houses the magnetometer, and the right tip supports the pitot tube, while both tips have LED Navigation/Strobe/Position lights attached to them. Mounting structures were designed, constructed, and installed to support the magnetometer and pitot tube. The pitot tube mount was a fun project that allowed us to explore some new fabrication methods. Some commercially available options exist for pitot tube mounts, but none of these integrate with our wing in an elegant way. We experimented with several different concepts but settled on a design that used an airfoil section made from a few layers of carbon fiber. In production we are considering a 3D printed pitot mount that is designed specifically for the DarkAero 1. All of the electrical hardware in the wing tips had to remain serviceable so we designed and built access panels into both of the wing tip covers. Once the magnetometer and pitot tube were installed and the wiring for the wing tip lights was routed, we were able to bond the wing tips in place. The bond line was lightly match-sanded which smoothed out the transition between the wingtip and the wing into a seamless, united assembly. We really liked seeing the wing tip lights mounted and powered on for the first time. We think they will look even better in the air!

Composites Course
We get asked about our mold patterns, molds, and composites work frequently. The DarkAero Knowledge Base and our YouTube channel give good introductions to these topics, however, they do not provide all the specific details on how to make quality end products. To meet these detailed questions and to help support our mission to create the DarkAero 1, we have developed a two-day composites course that we first announced in August. This course will cover a broad range of composite topics and includes hands-on demonstrations. The first session will be held in October and has already filled up! With the amount of interest we received, we are planning to hold a second session, either in November or early 2022. The second session has already started to fill up. Looking Ahead
In September we plan to continue with fabrication and installation of the main landing gear. In addition to fully assembling the primary structures for the gear, there are many smaller hardware and electrical components we will be pulling together to build up the retract system. We are planning to wrap up the integration of the electrical system into the fuselage as well.
